Can these connect into one model?

Yes — and they were clearly built to. Invoice and Pivot both carry item + customer + date keys that join to Master-item and Master-debtor; Inventory/PO/Batches join on item + supplier + warehouse. A single shared Date table ties facts and targets together. This is a textbook star schema waiting to be wired up.

⚠ The one catch to confirm

Keys are stored as "code, name" strings (e.g. 100073, Salt n Pepper). They must be split to a clean numeric code before relationships will hold. Confirm with the client that the leading code is the true primary key in every master.

02

Business Overall — the executive view

There is no single "Business Overall" sheet, but the executive layer is fully derivable: Invoice + Pivot give revenue, margin and volume; the Targets family gives the goalposts; AR adds cash health; Inventory adds asset/stock health. An executive dashboard is a roll-up of the department facts against target — not a new data source.

Company KPIs
  • Total Revenue, Total Margin, Margin % (VND)
  • Revenue & Margin vs Target (achievement %)
  • YoY / vs Last Year growth (Forecast sheet supports this)
  • Total Volume (kg) and Wagyu volume share
  • Active customers & new-vs-returning split
  • Receivables outstanding & % overdue (>90 days)
  • Stock value on hand & expiry-at-risk value
Executive pages to build
  • Company Scorecard — KPI band + revenue-vs-target trend
  • Department Comparison — Sales / Warehouse / AR side by side
  • Region & Channel — performance map of North/Central/South + channel mix
  • Cash & Stock Health — AR aging + inventory value & expiry
Management insights this layer unlocks

Are we hitting the monthly revenue & margin target? Which region or channel is carrying (or dragging) the number? Is growth coming from volume or price/mix? How much margin are we giving away through promo / FOC / compensation (visible only in Pivot)? Is cash tied up in overdue receivables or in slow, near-expiry stock? Those five questions are the whole executive story.

04

Deep dive — Sales priority

Sales is the strongest part of this dataset — two complementary fact tables, full customer & product dimensions, and targets at company / rep / region level. Below: what each analysis the brief asked for can or cannot be answered with the data in hand.

Fact A · Invoice (101,604 rows)

Gross invoice lines. Fields: INVOICE#, DATE, DEBTOR, ITEM, INVOICE REF, QUANTITY, PRICE/UNIT, REVENUE, COGS (per unit), COGS Total, MARGIN TOTAL. This is your order & revenue grain.

Fact B · Pivot (68,299 rows)

Net sales decomposed by transaction type: regular sales, promo, FOC (free-of-charge), replacement, compensation, sponsor, clearing, received-difference, authorities — each with VND & quantity, plus COGS & margin. This is how you measure revenue leakage.

Decide the relationship up front: Invoice and Pivot overlap. Confirm with the client whether Pivot is the authoritative net-sales table (regular + adjustments) and Invoice is gross billing — or vice-versa. Don't sum both into one "Total Sales" or you'll double-count. My recommended default: Pivot = net sales / margin truth, Invoice = order & pricing detail.

Opportunities visible in the data
  • Quantify margin leakage (promo/FOC/compensation) by customer — likely a quick profit win.
  • Identify top-20 customers driving most margin (HORECA names like Metropole, InterContinental appear in AR).
  • Push Wagyu mix — highest price/kg category — where volume is low.
  • Rank sales reps on margin %, not just revenue.
Risks / issues visible in the data
  • Negative net-sales values in Pivot — sign convention must be confirmed (returns vs sales).
  • Targets don't cover Jan–May 2025 while invoices do → achievement % will look blank.
  • Sales-rep names "Ha" vs "Ha ", "Hoa" vs "Hoa " split a rep into two.
  • Customer concentration / credit risk overlaps with overdue AR.

06

Recommended Power BI data model

Star schema, single direction, one shared Date table. Facts in the middle, conformed dimensions around them, targets attached at their natural grain.

Dim_Customer
Master-debtor · rep, region, channel
Dim_Product
Master-item · cat, brand, grade
Dim_Supplier
Master-supplier
Fact_Sales (Pivot)
net sales · margin · type
Fact_Invoice
orders · price · qty
Fact_Inventory · Fact_PO
stock · expiry · receipts
Fact_AR (aging snapshot)
Dim_Date
marked date table
Targets
company / rep / region
RLS_Users / Pages
security layer
all relationships single-direction → from dimension (1) to fact (∗) · Date filters every fact & target
Keys & relationships
  • Dim_Customer[DebtorCode] 1→∗ Fact_Sales / Fact_Invoice / Fact_AR
  • Dim_Product[Itemcode] 1→∗ Fact_Sales / Invoice / Inventory / PO
  • Dim_Supplier[SupplierCode] 1→∗ Inventory / PO / Product
  • Dim_Date[Date] 1→∗ every fact & every target table
  • Targets join on Date (+ rep/region key) — at month grain, so use a Month key or relate Date(month-start).
Modelling decisions
  • Build a dedicated Dim_Date (CALENDAR), mark as date table — don't rely on auto date/time.
  • Pick one sales fact as the revenue source of truth (recommend Pivot); keep the other for detail, don't double-count.
  • Inventory & AR are snapshots — relate to Date as "as-of", don't sum across time.
  • RLS tables drive security roles, not visuals — wire Users→Pages with role filters.
  • Region Sales mixes geography + channel — split into Region and Channel attributes.

DAX measures (Sales-first)

Assumes Fact_Sales = cleaned Pivot, Fact_Invoice = cleaned Invoice, conformed Dim_Date. Replace negative-sign on net sales once the convention is confirmed (wrap in ABS() or negate as agreed).
Total Sales
Net revenue, all transaction types · KPI cards, every trend.
Total Sales =
SUM ( Fact_Sales[total - vnd] )
Total Margin & Margin %
Profit and profitability · KPI cards, rep/customer ranking.
Total Margin = SUM ( Fact_Sales[Total Margin] )
Margin % = DIVIDE ( [Total Margin], [Total Sales] )
Total Orders & Volume
Order count & kg sold · overview KPIs.
Total Orders = DISTINCTCOUNT ( Fact_Invoice[INVOICE#] )
Total Volume (kg) = SUM ( Fact_Sales[total - q] )
Average Order Value / Price per Kg
Basket & pricing efficiency · KPI cards, customer page.
Avg Order Value = DIVIDE ( [Total Sales], [Total Orders] )
Avg Price / Kg  = DIVIDE ( [Total Sales], [Total Volume (kg)] )
Revenue Target & Achievement %
Goal vs actual · target gauges, vs-target combo chart.
Revenue Target = SUM ( Targets[Revenue target VND] )
Target Achievement % =
DIVIDE ( [Total Sales], [Revenue Target] )
MoM & YoY Growth %
Momentum · trend tooltips, growth KPI. YoY needs ≥13 months.
Sales MoM % =
VAR Prev = CALCULATE ( [Total Sales],
    DATEADD ( Dim_Date[Date], -1, MONTH ) )
RETURN DIVIDE ( [Total Sales] - Prev, Prev )

Sales YoY % =
VAR Prev = CALCULATE ( [Total Sales],
    DATEADD ( Dim_Date[Date], -1, YEAR ) )
RETURN DIVIDE ( [Total Sales] - Prev, Prev )
MTD & YTD Sales
Period-to-date · executive & overview headers.
MTD Sales = TOTALMTD ( [Total Sales], Dim_Date[Date] )
YTD Sales = TOTALYTD ( [Total Sales], Dim_Date[Date] )
Sales Contribution %
Share of total (works on any dimension) · mix bars, treemaps.
Sales Contribution % =
DIVIDE ( [Total Sales],
    CALCULATE ( [Total Sales], ALLSELECTED () ) )
Margin Leakage — promo / FOC / compensation ★ signature measure
Revenue given away vs regular sales · the Margin & Leakage page. Unique to this dataset.
Regular Sales = SUM ( Fact_Sales[regular sales - vnd] )
Promo+FOC Value =
SUM ( Fact_Sales[promo - vnd] ) + SUM ( Fact_Sales[FOC - vnd] )
    + SUM ( Fact_Sales[compensation - vnd] )
Leakage % of Sales =
DIVIDE ( [Promo+FOC Value], [Regular Sales] )
Wagyu Revenue Share
Premium-mix push · overview KPI, product page.
Wagyu Revenue Share =
DIVIDE (
  CALCULATE ( [Total Sales], Dim_Product[Category] = "Wagyu" ),
  [Total Sales] )
Active & Repeat Customers
Customer base & retention · customer page.
Active Customers =
DISTINCTCOUNT ( Fact_Sales[DebtorCode] )

Repeat Customers =
COUNTROWS ( FILTER ( VALUES ( Dim_Customer[DebtorCode] ),
    CALCULATE ( DISTINCTCOUNT ( Fact_Invoice[INVOICE#] ) ) > 1 ) )
Customer Contribution % (Pareto)
Who drives the revenue · top-N table, concentration.
Customer Contribution % =
DIVIDE ( [Total Sales],
  CALCULATE ( [Total Sales], ALL ( Dim_Customer ) ) )
AR Outstanding & % Overdue
Cash health · accounting + executive pages.
Total AR = SUM ( Fact_AR[Total amount] )
% Overdue 90+ =
DIVIDE ( SUM ( Fact_AR[>90] ), [Total AR] )
Near-Expiry Stock Value
Spoilage risk · warehouse page.
Near-Expiry Value =
CALCULATE ( SUM ( Fact_Inventory[Stock value] ),
  Expdate[End] <= TODAY () + 30 )
10

Power Query transformation steps

An ordered recipe — applied per query, then the model is wired in §6.

  1. Connect each sheet as its own query; promote headers.
  2. Set data types explicitly as step 1 (Date, Decimal, Whole, Text).
  3. Remove empty rows & columns (blank1, blank2, null-header cols).
  4. Trim + Clean all text key columns (Salesrep, Category, Itemtype, Temp zone).
  5. Split composite keys "code, name" → Code (number) + Name (text) by first delimiter.
  6. Standardise text: Capitalize Each Word; map Temp zone to Dry/Frozen/Chilled.
  1. Mapping tables for category clean-up ("Diary"→"Dairy") via Merge.
  2. Fix dates: convert datetime→Date (resolve UTC+7 shift first).
  3. Confirm sign on Pivot net-sales / Inventory value; standardise.
  4. Remove duplicates on master keys only (after trim).
  5. Create Dim_Date (CALENDAR / List.Dates) covering all facts + targets; mark as date table.
  6. Split Region/Channel out of "Region Sales"; don't load the Check sheet.

Append only if more monthly files arrive in the same shape; Merge is for enriching facts with master attributes if you prefer flattening over relationships (relationships recommended here).

Build priority

P1
Sales Dashboard. Richest, cleanest data (two fact tables + full dimensions + multi-level targets), it's the client's stated focus, and it carries a unique margin-leakage angle that demos extremely well. Fastest path to visible value.
P2
Business Overall Dashboard. Reuses the Sales measures plus AR & inventory roll-ups — cheap to build once Sales exists, and it's what leadership asks for first.
P3
Warehouse Dashboard. High operational value (expiry & stock-value risk on perishables) but snapshot-only data; useful even without history.
P4
Accounting / AR Dashboard. Quick win but narrow (aging snapshot only). Best folded into the executive page until more finance data arrives.
12

Final recommendation

What this data is

A premium imported-meat & seafood distributor in Vietnam (Wagyu-led, HORECA + retail). The workbook is already a near-complete Power BI source: facts, dimensions, targets and RLS.

Build first & why

Sales — best data, client priority, and a standout margin-leakage story competitors' dashboards rarely show.

Most important KPIs

Revenue & Margin vs Target, Margin %, Growth, Wagyu share, Promo/FOC leakage, Top customers & reps by margin.

Fix before building

Composite keys, sales-rep trailing spaces, category typos, the date UTC shift, and the Pivot/Invoice double-count + sign convention.

Data that would level it up
  • ≥13 months history (true YoY)
  • Targets for the full date range (Jan–May 2025 gap)
  • List price / discount to measure true discounting
  • Dated stock-movement ledger (turnover, trend)
  • AP & cash to round out finance
  • Customer first-order / onboarding dates
Questions to ask the client first
  1. Is Pivot or Invoice the official sales number? How do they differ?
  2. Why are net-sales values negative — sign convention or returns?
  3. How far back does history go / will this refresh monthly?
  4. Confirm the leading numeric code is the true key in each master.
  5. What does each transaction type mean (FOC, compensation, sponsor, authorities)?
  6. Who sees what? (validate the RLS Users/Pages mapping)
  7. Currency display — VND, or VND + USD reporting view?
